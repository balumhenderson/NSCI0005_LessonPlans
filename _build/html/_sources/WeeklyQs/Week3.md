### 1. a) Find he derivative of $(x^3+2x)\sin(x)$

```{admonition} Solution
:class: dropdown, seealso
Using the chain rule ($v\mathrm{d}u + v\mathrm{d}v$) with $u = x^3+2x$ and $v = sin(x)$, we get:

$(3x^2 + 2)\sin(x) + (x^3+2x)\cos(x)$
```
